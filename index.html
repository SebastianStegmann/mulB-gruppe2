Before you start the setup

If you intend to change the gauge of your strings, you should skip to the strings/cleaning section first, and then follow the guide. Otherwise just do everything in the order listed.

Before you start, go ahead and tune up your guitar. Every time you tune your guitar, you should have it in the position you play it in, not lying down, where you may have it while working on it.

Tools needed

  • Allen key for trussrod
  • Allen key for saddle adjustment
  • Small screwdriver for pickups
  • Fretboard oil
  • Microfiber cloth
  • Toothpick or a thin pick

Check the truss rod

Put a capo on fret 1, and press down on the highest fret with one finger. Now take your other finger and press down in the middle of the string.

You want it to have a tiny bit of give. Small enough to fit a business card. Too much give, and the neck needs to be tightened. No give at all, then you loosen it.

Rotate clockwise to loosen, counter clockwise to tighten.

1

Fix your action

This will look different depending on which guitar you have, but should be easy to figure out.

You will have either individual saddles, compensated saddles or a two post bridge. Lower it to your preference, but never let the string be parallel to the board. The string should be higher at the 12th fret, than at the 1st.

2

Intonate

Play the note at the 12th fret, and then play the 12th fret harmonic. Adjust the fretted note, to the harmonic.

If the fretted note is too low, shorten the string, by pushing the saddle a bit forward. Pull the saddle back if the note is too high. The longer the string, the lower the note. The shorter the string, the higher the note.

Ideally make use of a tuner.

3

Noodle

To test if your adjust was done correctly, play your guitar.

Play every note on the guitar, see if they all ring out. Bend the strings, feel if it’s comfortable. Play chords all over the neck, to check intonation.

Remember, there isn’t one correct answer, if you like it, then it’s good.

4

New strings & a clean guitar

When the setup is done and you have taken off your old strings, follow these steps:

1. Wipe down fretboard, around the pickups and near the bridge. Remove all dust and grime.
2. Take your toothpick and scrape softly around the frets, to get all gunk off.
3. Apply fretboard oil according to instructions on the bottle, and let dry.
4. Put on the new strings, and stretch them.

Now your guitar is ready to play again!

Other tips

Over time the guitar needs a bit of maintinence, even though it has been through a proper setup. You should look for loose strap buttons, and make sure to tighten them.

Your output jack can also wiggle and become loose with time, make sure to tighten in, so that your sound doesn’t cut out.

Adjust pickup height, probably lower than you expect. A good tip is to have the bass side of the pickup lower than the treble side, to compensate for the size and output of the thicker strings.

Lastly, check that nothing on your tuners is loose. Beware, do not over tighten.
